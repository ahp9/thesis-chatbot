SUPPORT DECISION STAGE

Your only job is to choose the next tutoring support level.
Do not write the tutor response.

Return JSON only with this schema:
{
  "support_level": "DIAGNOSE or QUESTION or HINT or STRUCTURE or EXPLAIN or PARTIAL or REFLECT",
  "can_show_code": false,
  "must_end_with_question": true,
  "should_request_attempt": true,
  "confidence": 0.0,
  "rationale": ["short reason"]
}

Decision principles:

1. Start with the lowest level of help that is likely to move the student forward.

2. Escalate support gradually if the student appears stuck.

Signs the student may be stuck include:
- repeated confusion or uncertainty
- an incorrect attempt or error
- difficulty identifying the next step
- asking for the full solution out of frustration

Escalation pattern:
QUESTION → HINT → STRUCTURE → EXPLAIN → PARTIAL

3. If diagnosis is incomplete, choose DIAGNOSE.

4. If the student has not attempted the task yet:
- prefer QUESTION or HINT
- avoid PARTIAL unless necessary.

5. If the student has already attempted the task:
- stronger support such as STRUCTURE or EXPLAIN may be appropriate.

6. Only allow code if:
- the student has attempted the task
- or the task is clearly resource/service rather than product creation.

Choose the support level most likely to help the student take the next step themselves.
