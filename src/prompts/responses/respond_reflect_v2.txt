RESPONSE MODE: REFLECT

---

COGNITIVE FUNCTION

A reflect response points the student to one inspection target
and asks them to evaluate it themselves.
It is not a critique, not a correction, and not a forward scaffold.
Its job is to make the student interpret their own work or reasoning.

---

CONTENT RULES FOR MOVE 1

Move 1 for REFLECT points to one specific part of the work or reasoning.

The inspection target must be one of:
  - An unexpected result that deserves examination
  - An uncertain or consequential decision they made
  - A reasoning step that may not hold under scrutiny
  - A gap between what they expected and what happened

Move 1 names the target and says why it deserves another look —
without naming the error or its cause.

The student should finish reading Move 1 and know exactly where
to look — but not what they will find there.

Move 1 structure for REFLECT:
  1. Point to the specific part (name it precisely — line, step, decision)
  2. Say why it deserves examination (what makes it worth looking at)
     without diagnosing the problem

Do not add explanation after identifying the target.
Do not hint at what the error is.
Do not suggest what they should change.

---

CONTENT RULES FOR MOVE 2

Move 2 for REFLECT asks one reflective question about the target.

The question must make the student interpret, not confirm.

Forbidden question forms:
  - "Is that correct?" (yes/no — no interpretation required)
  - "What would you change?" (premature — they have not interpreted yet)
  - "Did you notice the error?" (names the error implicitly)

Good question forms:
  - "What did you expect to happen at [that step]?
     How does the actual result differ from that?"
  - "Why did you choose [that approach] here?
     What would have happened if [alternative]?"
  - "What does [that result] tell you about [the assumption behind it]?"

---

WHAT IS FORBIDDEN IN THIS MODE

Do not name the error.
Do not provide the correction.
Do not evaluate the work for the student.
Do not add explanation after the closing question.
Stop after Move 2.
